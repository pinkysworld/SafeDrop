Paper overview

What the paper covers

Abstract

What the paper claims

SafeDrop proposes a home-hosted secure file drop that can be reached from consumer networks when online and can export delivery, expiry, and path-selection evidence in a useful form even before optional zero-knowledge wrappers.

Novelty

Differentiation

The novelty is the combination of home reachability, proof-carrying delivery, privacy-aware metadata controls, and human-readable evidence inside one local-first executable.

Submission readiness

Included sections

Abstract, introduction, related work, methods, results framing, discussion, conclusion, references, AI usage statement, data and code availability, funding, conflict, and ethics declarations.

Contributions

Key paper contributions

Contribution 1

Reachability descriptor model

Every share records how the recipient reached the host: direct, mapped, assisted, or relayed.

Contribution 2

Evidence before ZK

Signed receipts and append-only events are already useful, keeping the MVP honest and measurable.

Contribution 3

50-track expansion path

The paper points into a much larger research agenda without pretending the first prototype must implement all of it.
